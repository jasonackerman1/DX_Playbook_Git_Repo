What is Business Thinking?

Growth Mindset
Deep Dive
Definition Is curious and embraces challenges as learning opportunities. Acknowledges and takes ownership for own areas of development by seeking feedback from others to continuously grow. Believes that abilities and talent are assets that everyone can develop with effort and perseverance benefitting the organization as a whole.
Behavior
  • Self-reflects and actively seeks opportunities to develop, share knowledge and support others to grow
  • Sees challenges and failures/mistakes as opportunities to learn and grow, showing perseverance in the face of difficulties
  • Seeks feedback as a way of continuous development
Mindset
We need less of this Mindset

No need to learn new things «I am already an Expert in my field”

“If I fail, others will think that I am not skilled or competent”

“This is the way we do things around here”

We need more of this Mindset

“Learning and growing is an ongoing journey, it only takes personal commitment and perseverance”

“If it did not work the way I planned, it is not a failure but an opportunity for me to learn”

“What does not kill you, makes you stronger”

Keywords
  • #Curiosity
  • #Learning
  • #Feedback
What is in it for Me / My Team / KM
  • Me: Personal and professional development, expanding knowledge and perspectives, gaining more confidence
  • My Team: Team growth based on feedback, knowledge sharing, support in face of failures, greater team flexibility 
  • KM:  Build internal people capabilities, foster a learning organisation, increase agility, adaptability and growth 
Real People Stories

Story 1. Growth Mindset

Situation: One of our colleagues took on the chance of a foreign assignment, even if it an easy decision - stepping into the unknown, in a very new program, and relocating with family and a small daughter.

Mindset & Behavior: “What led me to the decision to take on the assignment was in fact the possible growth of myself as a Konica Minolta employee – as a person and also as a professional.”

Outcome and Learnings: He quickly felt comfortable in his new environment and appreciated the chance to engage with different people from various countries and make experiences that he wouldn't have been able to make in his local NOC. “The challenge made me grow. From a professional point of view, it was nice to be on the other side of the fence and having other projects and challenges to work on. I was supporting my local colleagues and now I'm supporting a much wider range, that's a special plus. Looking at the future, I would regret to not have taken the chance.”


Story 2. Growth Mindset

Situation: One of our customers wanted support towards their digital transformation. The colleague dealing with that customer had only a few months ahead of him before meeting again the customer to explore solutions on a topic he was not familiar with. He had a clear goal before that meeting: learning as much as possible about Digital Transformation.

Mindset & Behavior: He used a network of internal colleagues to find which different ways he could take to learn more about the topic and have a deep knowledge in order to help his client. He entered MPlus Learning Management System and searched for the “Digital Transformation” videos to expand his understanding on the topic. He researched internally and externally using all sort of sources from our different partners allowing interesting starting points for collaboration with customers but also with colleagues. With all this new acquired knowledge, he thought of how Konica Minolta solutions can improve services for our customer's customers and products.

Outcome and Learnings: After a few weeks, he felt much more comfortable with the new topic, he gained confidence in his inner talent/learning capacity/capability and he was able toengage in a fruitful and powerful discussion with the customer. There is no topic broad enough that he cannot learn from and grow.

Anchors/Proficiency Levels (Builds upon previous levels)
  1. Emerging ⓘ
    • Demonstrates curiosity for new topics and ways of doing things.
    • Is aware of own strengths and development areas.
    • Understands feedback as a way of improvement and gains key learnings to enhance own skills and behavior.
    • Learns from mistakes and shows perseverance in the face of dfficulties.

  2. Evolving ⓘ
    • Self-reflects and actively seeks opportunities or new challenges to learn and grow.
    • Asks for feedback on performance, behavior, strengths and development areas and uses it to improve and develop.
    • Shows perseverance in the face of obstacles and derives learnings from each experience.
    • Is humble in approach to learning. Understands that we cannot know it all, but we can keep on learning more.

  3. Influencing ⓘ
    • Continuously engages a growth mindset over all work activities.
    • Demonstrates resilience and persisistence when facing and overcoming challenges.
    • Is willing to share examples of success and failures tied to lessons learned.
    • Supports others in their development, e.g. by actively giving feedback, by supporting them to identify development opportunities and/or by taking the role of a mentor or coach.
    • Is humble in own approach to personal and professional growth and engages with others to learn with and through them, leveraging their different views and perspectives.

  4. Transforming ⓘ
    • Actively promotes and inspires others to engage a growth mindset over all work activities.
    • Acts as a role model for relentless perseverance and encourages others to do the same.
    • Creates a culture around them where people are invited to share mistakes so that everyone can learn and improve from them.
    • Is humble in own approach to learning and performing at work, willing to reconsider and adjust own view and practices to become better and ensure continuous improvement.
