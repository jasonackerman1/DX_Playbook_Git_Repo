Leadership Driver: Strategic Thinking

Engages with internal/external customers as a partner and identifies opportunities to add value and improve their experience.

  2. Leadership Drivers
  3. Strategic Thinking
What is Strategic Thinking?

Customer Centricity
Deep Dive
Definition Engages with internal and external customers as a partner by listening to and understanding their latent and evolving needs. Takes customers' business, environmental and social sustainability challenges into consideration and co-creates innovative and tailored solutions together. Identifies opportunities to improve customer experience, creates added value and enhances customer engagement.
Behavior
  • Works with internal and/or external customers to assess their needs in an effort to exceed their expectations
  • Anticipates the expectations and needs of internal and/or external customers and engages with them in order to add value
  • Explores different approaches and presents solutions to problems to generate added value to internal and/or external customers
Mindset
We need less of this Mindset

“I know my customer better than anybody else”

“This is my customer, not yours so do not interfere”

“I have a portfolio of products and solutions to sell and I have always been successful”

We need more of this Mindset

“Share your experience with me, there might be something new for me to explore that would benefit my customer”

“What is better for the customer? Let's think about the benefit for them, not for us only” 

“My focus is on understanding the customer's business and challenges so we can find and shape solutions together”

What is in it for Me / My Team / KM
  • Me: Be a successful trusted partner, increased level of engagement and sense of belonging
  • My Team: Improve team engagement, trust, enhance group dynamics 
  • KM:  Better customer experience, increased levels of internal and external customers' engagement, loyalty, higher business results 
Real People Stories

Story 1. Customer Centricity (Internal Customer)

Situation: One colleague started to work in a global project and decided to use Microsoft Teams to exchange rapidly and regularly with her team. Unfortunately, some technical issues made it difficult for a member to join the team virtually. The issue was raised but as no satisfactory solution was brought, the issue was raised a second time to Mike, another colleague of the same department.

Mindset & Behavior: Rather than skipping the topic as not part of his job description, Mike asked questions to understand the situation and he took charge of the problem. He liaised proactively with different internal customers and checked regularly with all parts involved until he solved the issue.

Outcome and Learnings: The team could rapidly make progress on the global project and ensure that all regional perspectives were taken into account for a final buy in. The team felt strongly supported and engaged to continue working together.


Story 2. Customer Centricity (External Customer)

Situation: At an international fair, a large customer shared with one of our colleagues in Sales their concerns about the increase of digit passwords in their Security Requirements. That was just an open conversation, there was no explicit request from the customer to get a solution from our Sales colleague. Far from being just a random discussion, our Sales colleague decided to work on the customer's issue.

Mindset & Behavior: He took charge of the Customer Issue even when not explicitly asked to do it: He then engaged proactively with internal and external network, across regions and functions and gathered multiple views and perspectives to the customer's issue for better solution identification. He thought of different solutions and options and what those mean in terms of implications for the customer and finally, he invited the customer to join the project and explore the different options together in order to support him with shaping the solution together.

Outcome and Learnings: A co-created solution fully owned by the customer and our Sales colleague; evolving relationship between the customer and our Sales colleague to a trusting partnership.

Anchors/Proficiency Levels (Builds upon previous levels)
  1. Emerging ⓘ
    • Builds and maintains open and trustworthy relationships with internal and external customers.
    • Explores customer needs with genuine curiosity and interest in adding customer value.
    • Seeks to understand the customer's issue by asking open questions and uses this information to formulate customer concerns clearly and deliver a timely and suitable solution.
    • Listens to the customer's concerns, needs and expectations and incorporates these in to service delivery approach.
    • Seeks agreement on customer expectations to ensure a good customer experience.

  2. Evolving ⓘ
    • Understands the customer's business, industry, brand and environment social sustainability challenges and knows who to involve to provide a solution.
    • Elicits on going feedback from customers to create strategies to help the organization provide better service.
    • Considers both short and long-term needs and interests of the customer and invites the customer to co-shape a suitable solution.
    • Maintains accountability to make sure customer needs are met, collaborating with internal and external stakeholders to ensure deliverables and deadlines are met.

  3. Influencing ⓘ
    • Actively listens to customers needs and concerns and incorporates into opportunities to deliver more meaningful products and services
    • Leverages best practices in determining sustainable solutions while customizing approach to the unique needs of the customer.
    • Explores fresh customer solutions and debates their value before settling on an option in collaboration with the client.
    • Uses tangible methods to monitor and measure customer satisfaction and proactively takes action to improve it.
    • Consistently considers opportunities to help the broader organization provide value to the customer.

  4. Transforming ⓘ
    • Has a deep understanding of industry trends, complex customer requirements and incorporates this information in creating and presenting solutions to enhance the customer's business.
    • Takes an “out of the box” approach and embraces risk as an opportunity to innovate and enhace the customer experience.
    • Creates a customer centric culture by ensuring that the customer is the focal point of all decisions relating to delivering product and services.
    • Fosters customer-centric behavior within the organization by continuously reinforcing the impact of employee actions on the customer experience.
    • Empowers employees by providing them with data and resources so they can consider the best approach in supporting the business and customer
    • Shares customer success stories to inspire others to consider how they can provide customer value.
