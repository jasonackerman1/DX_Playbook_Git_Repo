Leadership Driver: Building Allaiances

Builds relationships, works together with others towards common goals and uses networking effectively.

  1. DX Playbook
  2. Leadership Drivers
  3. Building Alliances
What is Building Alliances?
Earn completion in Success Factors Coming Soon!
Collaboration
Deep Dive
Definition Builds and fosters collaboration inside and outside the organization; works together with others towards common goals to contribute to team and organisational performance; supports individual's and team's success by breaking silos, sharing information, connecting people and mobilising key resources.
Behavior
  • Works with internal and/or external customers to assess their needs in an effort to exceed their expectations
  • Anticipates the expectations and needs of internal and/or external customers and engages with them in order to add value
  • Explores different approaches and presents solutions to problems to generate added value to internal and/or external customers
Mindset
We need less of this Mindset

“Working in silos, not sharing information with team members or colleagues”

“I don't have time to support others, I have so much to do”

““Sharing and collaborating with colleagues outside of my workspace is complicated and takes time, I prefer to work only with those seating in the same physical environment””

We need more of this Mindset

“Approaches new ways of working by making use of communities and networks, groups of peers or virtual teams for example Teams, SharePoint”

“Contributes and shares best practice in support of success” 

“I am working on this project, who could I reach out in the organization to share ideas and best practice?”

“How I can contribute to my team or colleagues' success?”

What is in it for Me / My Team / KM
  • Me: Peer support, share best practice, speed of information, real time exchange and feedback
  • My Team: Reach out to others and share best practice; utilise skills and experience 
  • KM:  Break silos, leverage synergies, knowledge sharing across regions, faster time to market and customers 
Real People Stories

Story 1. Collaboration

Situation: One of our top ten largest hospital customers in one of our regions was seeking a vendor who could provide them with a plan for fleet optimization, better utilization of their print shop, and overall reduction on print spend.

Mindset & Behavior: The Direct Sales, Service, Solutions, BIS and Engagement teams all needed to work together, along with our Bid desk, to offer a response that was aligned to this hospital's goals and initiatives. These teams worked to complete the assessment plan as well as present our managed deployment methodology. Over 8 days, these teams worked together to assess over 1,800 devices and provide a comprehensive solution to this customer, resulting in the deployment of over 600 devices, a Papercut solution installed on over 100 devices, increased productivity and reduces costs for this customer. Together, these teams worked to truly understand the needs of the customer and offer a comprehensive solution to address those needs.

Outcome and Learnings: This KM team worked together to not only partner to propose a solution to meet the customer's needs, but to regard Konica Minolta as a trusted business partner.


Story 2. Collaboration

Situation: A colleague was asked to lead a stretch project and redesign a programme/process that they had minimum experience in. Despite conducting extensive online research, they still couldn't decide on the right solution.

Mindset & Behavior: Rather than trying to make a decision on their own, the colleague decided that it would be better to approach the project through strength in numbers. The colleague ran focus groups with individuals who had recently been through the programme/process, they reached out to other colleagues in different regions to see what they were currently doing. In addition to sharing and collating ideas from other members of the organisation they decided that it would also be a good idea to reach out to their external network, they attended specific network events, created LinkedIn polls and actively seeked out experienced professionals in this area who they had worked with previously.

Outcome and Learnings: Not only did our colleague and extended team created a much better programme/process in-house but they utilised the skills from a variety of sources to create a best-practice programme/process. This in turn resulted in being recognised externally and being nominated for awards within our industry.

Anchors/Proficiency Levels (Builds upon previous levels)
  1. Emerging ⓘ
    • “Understands that building internal networks and establishing relationships at work are important.”
    • “Regularly shares information within the team and stays abreast of the activities of others.”
    • “Makes use of virtual tools to work with others on shared objectives, knowledge share and gather information to learn from others often beyond their own business area.”
    • “Places higher priority on achieving team goals than own personal agenda.”
    • “Is honest and transparent in all interactions, to build strong working relationships.”

  2. Evolving ⓘ
    • “Acts as a connector, readily seeking and identifying individuals across the organisation who can enable better joint business solutions.”
    • “Communicates clear and concisely at a all levels, makes use of active listening to ensure everyone is heard.”
    • “Exchanges viewpoints, ideas and information with others and asks for their feedback”
    • “Motivates, supports and recognises others in achieving common business goals.”

  3. Influencing ⓘ
    • “Overcomes boundaries, barriers or silos within the organisation, encouraging network and knowledge sharing to achieve common goals.”
    • “Advocates the importance of building and maintaining internal networks, encouraging teams and colleagues to do the same at all levels in the organisation.”
    • “Actively seeks different perspectives as a basis to make solid decisions, creating environments for others to share their knowledge and viewpoints.”
    • “Motivates others to support each other, strive at work and celebrate each other's success.”

  4. Transforming ⓘ
    • “Demonstrates interpersonal sensitivity, curiosity, and diplomacy by effectively adjusting their own style among diverse stakeholders, functions, people and work environments.”
    • “Inspires others to create collaborative working environments and new ways of working together to achieve shared business goals.”
    • “Personally engages in promoting collaboration, team spirit, and networking at Konica Minolta.”
    • “Uses digital platforms available to them to bring people together, and celebrate others successes openly across the business.”
    • “Proactively builds long-term working relationships within and beyond the organisation to advance shared goals.”
